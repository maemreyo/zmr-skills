## 3. Intended Level

### Official Stance

The book's own front matter states:

> *"Mindset for IELTS Foundation Level is aimed at students who are thinking about taking IELTS, but who are currently at an A2 level."*
> — Official PDF, page 10 (via flipbuilder)

Cambridge official shop lists **CEFR A2**.

### Level Descriptions from Various Sources

| Source | Claimed Level | Wording |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| Cambridge official shop | A2 | "Designed for students currently at level A2" |
| Hwa Tai (Taiwan distributor) | A2–B1 / "below IELTS Band 5.5" | "Elementary to Pre-intermediate" |
| Goodreads | A2–B1 | CEF level range for Foundation |
| CUP Teaching Guide (Scribd) | "Initial preparation only" | No band target assigned |
| IOSR Journal paper | Band 3.0 | "for learners at band level 3.0" |
| PREP Edu | Band 4.5 | "target band 4.5" |
| Various teacher blogs | Band 3.0–4.5 | Range of estimates |

**Verdict**: Cambridge officially says A2 with NO target band score. The band targets (3.0–4.5) are teacher/third-party estimates. The series header shows "FOUNDATION LEVEL" without a band number, while Levels 1–3 explicitly say "Target Band 5.5", "Target Band 6.5", "Target Band 7.5".

## 4. Course Structure

### Book Components

The course comprises 5 key components (per official PDF, page 8):

1. **Foundation Student's Book** (print and digital) — 10 topic-based units, 60–90 hours teaching
2. **Online Skills Modules** — 8 hours per skill per level
   - Listening and Speaking
   - Reading and Writing
   - Language Builder
   - Grammar and Vocabulary
3. **Academic Study Skills** — 6 hours (locked at Foundation Level)
4. **L1 Specific Modules** — 6 hours per module (Middle East, International)
5. **Online Practice Tests** — Testbank / Cambridge One

## 5. Unit 1: Daily Life — Complete Breakdown

### 5.1 Learning Outcomes (Official)

From the official PDF page 10 — verbatim:

> **IN THIS UNIT YOU WILL LEARN HOW TO**
> - understand activities that people do in their daily routine
> - use present simple and adverbs of frequency
> - read multiple texts to choose the correct answer
> - write describing a daily routine
> - read to guess meaning from context
> - listen to information about a student exchange trip
> - speak about your day

### 5.2 Section-by-Section Layout

#### [p12] Lead-in
- **Activity 1**: Look at pictures with labelled activities (get up, exercise, work, study, catch the bus, watch TV, go online, relax with friends, go to bed, have breakfast). Write 5 more activities you do every day.
- **Activity 2**: Pair work — compare answers and talk about what time you do each activity.

#### [p13] Vocabulary and Speaking
- **Activity 3** (audio 03): Read sentences about Ping (a boy on a language course in the UK). Choose A, B or C. Collocations practised: *gets up, catches the bus, chats to, come from, does sightseeing, watches TV*.
- **Activity 4**: Pair discussion — Do you do any of these activities? What time does your school/job start and finish? What do young people do in the evenings in your country?

#### [p14] Reading: Choose the Correct Answer
- **Activity 5**: Skim article about three teenagers' daily lives — Nina (Norway), Ava (Australia), Michael (Brazil). Underline activities you also do.
- **Activity 6**: 7 multiple-choice questions about the article. Tip boxes explain exam strategy (info appears in more than one text; look for paraphrasing).

#### [p15] Grammar and Speaking
- **Grammar box**: Present simple table (4 verb groups: regular, -s/-ch/-sh/-x, consonant+y, -o/-ss/-ch/-sh) + adverbs of frequency scale (never 0% → always 100%).
- **Activity 7**: Match questions 1–3 with answers A–C (use of present simple, purpose of adverbs of frequency, verb ending for he/she/it).
- **Activity 8**: Find examples of verb groups 1–4 in Lead-in and Reading texts.
- **Activity 9**: Pairs — one student closes book, other describes a teenager from the article; guess who.

#### [p16] Reading: Sentence Completion
- **Activity 10**: Sentence completion task (verb forms). Text about daily routine. Key answers: *gets up, has, eats, gets, goes, returns, makes, watches, meets, go, studies, goes, cooks, exercises, sees, falls*.

#### [p17] Writing: A Description
- **Activity 11**: Look at two pictures of Julia Grant (volunteer on a science project). Write description of her daily routine. Must begin "Julia Grant is 21 years old and works as a volunteer on a science project in Fiji. Every morning, she…" Use sequencing words (After that / Next / Then) and adverbs of frequency.
- **Activity 12**: Pairs — compare texts, correct each other's spelling/grammar/punctuation.

#### [p17] Listening: Matching
- **Activity 13**: True/False about a student exchange website (3 statements).
- **Activity 14** (audio 02): Listen to Carlos and Jack discussing an exchange trip. Identify their countries (Jack → Australia, Carlos → USA).
- **Activity 15** (audio 02): Match 5 family members to activities (A–H). Mini tips about paraphrasing and similar meanings.
- **Activity 16**: Pair/small group discussion about exchange trips.

#### [p18] Speaking: Talking About Your Day (IELTS Part 2 style)
- **Activity 17**: Read task. Spend 2 minutes thinking and making notes. "Describe what you usually do on an average day. You should say: what you do, who you do it with, where you do it. Explain what you enjoy most about your day and why."
- **Activity 18**: In pairs — Student A speaks 1–2 minutes, Student B evaluates (yes/no on 6 criteria: easy to understand, pauses/hesitates, stays on topic, too long/short, interesting). Swap roles.

#### [p19] Grammar and Vocabulary (End-of-unit practice)
- **Activity 1**: Match digital times (05:15, 08:20, etc.) to words (A–J).
- **Activity 2**: Unscramble letters to complete daily routine text (have, brush, meet, catch, leave, go, watch, do, go to, fall).
- **Activity 3**: Classify collocations into Make (a mess, lunch, the beds, dinner, a noise) or Do (some homework, the laundry, the shopping, the cleaning, the housework).

### 5.3 Grammar and Vocabulary Answer Key (Verbatim from Studocu answer key upload)

**Activity 1**: 1H, 2D, 3E, 4J, 5B, 6C, 7I, 8A, 9F, 10G

**Activity 2**: 1 have, 2 brush, 3 meet, 4 catch, 5 leave, 6 go, 7 watch, 8 do, 9 go to, 10 fall

**Activity 3**: Make → a noise, lunch, the beds, dinner, a mess | Do → some homework, the laundry, the shopping, the cleaning, the housework

**Activity 4** (present simple text): 1 live, 2 work, 3 get up, 4 leave, 5 have, 6 walk, 7 catch, 8 finish, 9 meet, 10 go out

**Activity 5** (3rd person -s): 1 washes, 2 watches, 3 cries, 4 practises, 5 goes, 6 does, 7 catches, 8 relaxes, 9 gives, 10 switches off

**Activity 6** (gap fill): 1 works, 2 play, 3 studies, 4 come, 5 wear, 6 like, 7 gets up, 8 have, 9 makes, 10 begins/finishes

**Activity 7** (sentence writing): 1 My friend tidies the room. 2 I play football at the weekend. 3 I take a sandwich to college for lunch. 4 I wake up at half past six. 5 My brother checks his phone every five minutes. 6 I have a shower in the evening. 7 I try to stop studying before 9pm. 8 I go to the dentist every six months. 9 My mother does most of the housework. 10 I visit my family at the weekends.

**Activity 8** (text completion): 1 work, 2 start, 3 opens, 4 fill, 5 tidy, 6 clean, 7 work, 8 tells, 9 shouts, 10 take, 11 finish, 12 gets, 13 enjoy

## 6. Unit 1 Skills & IELTS Alignment

| Skill | Activity Type | IELTS Connection |
|-------|---------------|------------------|
| Reading | Multiple choice (3 texts) | Part 1-style multiple choice; introduces scanning for information across texts |
| Reading | Sentence completion | Part 1-style gap-fill with word count awareness |
| Writing | Describe a daily routine | Foundation for Writing Part 1 (describing a process/schedule) and Part 2 (describing a personal routine) |
| Listening | Matching speakers to activities | Section 2-style matching task |
| Listening | True/False | Section 1-style comprehension |
| Speaking | Part 2 long turn (1–2 min) | Full IELTS Speaking Part 2 simulation with preparation time and peer evaluation |
| Grammar | Present simple + adverbs of frequency | Foundational for all IELTS bands |
| Vocabulary | Make/Do collocations, telling time, daily routine verbs | High-frequency functional vocabulary |

## 9. Methodology Notes

- **Search engines used**: Exa (primary), with fallback to Google-derived results via webfetch
- **Total distinct queries**: 15+ (variations on title, ISBN, edition, Unit 1 content, author names, level descriptors, series structure)
- **Languages searched**: English (primary), with some results in Spanish, Vietnamese, Chinese, German, Polish
- **Verification principle**: Every claim sourced from the highest-authority publicly available document. Where primary sources (official CUP pages) were behind login or 403, the next-best retailer/aggregator source is cited.
- **Edition ambiguity**: Counter-searched both ISBNs (9781316636688 and 9781009280280) and cross-referenced author lists to catch the Hwa Tai catalog error.
- **No copyrighted reproduction**: Exercise content is described structurally (activities, skills, topics) without reproducing full text of reading passages, listening scripts, or complete exercises.
