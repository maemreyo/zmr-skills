# Inverse Thinking

Use this only when a realistic misconception has a clear boundary.

1. **Disaster:** Show a specific, age-appropriate failure caused by the wrong choice; never use a generic “this is wrong.”
2. **Clues:** Have learners identify evidence that predicts the failure.
3. **Safe zone:** Contrast a context where the tempting choice is correct.
4. **Rule:** Compress the boundary into one decision rule.
5. **Transfer:** Apply the rule in a new context without copying the model.

Keep the pedagogical rationale teacher-only. For vocabulary, a disaster may be a plausible but socially or semantically wrong word choice; clues are meaning, register, or collocation. For grammar, a disaster may be a time-line contradiction; clues are markers and event order. If no meaningful misconception boundary exists, choose another method unless the teacher pinned inverse thinking; then explain the limitation and adapt without silently replacing it.
