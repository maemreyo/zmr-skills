# Board Plan Contract

The board is a student memory surface, not a transcript of the lesson. Use short labels, parallel examples, and one visible decision rule. A vocabulary board uses exactly: `word`, `core_meaning`, `selection_clues`, `collocations`, `memorable_example`. Keep entries compact enough for learners to copy during the lesson. Put teacher rationale, anticipated errors, answers, and follow-up notes outside the student board.

When materialized as an artifact, attach `artifact_id`, `artifact_type`, `audience`, positive `version`, `brief_id`, `grade_band`, optional `cefr`, `target_language`, `instruction_language`, `objective_ids`, `methodology_lineage`, `source_references`, `authority`, `accessibility`, `brand`, and versioned `dependencies`.
