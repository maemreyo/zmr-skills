# English Domain Playbooks

- Vocabulary: anchor core meaning, selection clues, collocations, register, and one memorable example; contrast only genuinely confusable words.
- Grammar: model meaning before form, show time or discourse context, contrast the smallest changed feature, and end with transfer.
- Pronunciation: name the articulatory or stress feature, provide hear–notice–produce cycles, and avoid implying one accent is the only legitimate English.
- Reading: set a purpose, locate evidence, distinguish literal from inferred meaning, and require an evidence-linked response.
- Writing: model the target move, annotate why it works, co-construct one example, then transfer to a new prompt.
- Listening: preview only essential context, use transcript clues when audio is absent, and separate gist, detail, and inference checks.
- Speaking: provide language frames, private or pair rehearsal, meaningful choice, and a retry after feedback.

All examples are age-appropriate, culturally safe, interesting, and short enough to write during class.
