# Difficulty and Rubrics

Difficulty is evidence demand, abstraction, integration, and independence—not rare vocabulary or confusing wording. Honor an explicit item count and difficulty distribution exactly. If none is supplied, choose and state a balanced distribution appropriate to the purpose; for a short exit ticket, prioritize direct objective evidence over artificial balance.

Analytic rubrics name observable criteria, performance levels, and points. Separate content/meaning, language control, organization, and evidence only when the objective requires them. Use learner-facing labels suitable for the grade band and teacher-facing scoring notes that identify acceptable variation. Record whether answers come from teacher authority, supplied sources, or provisional model judgment.
