# Error Recovery

When a specialist fails:

1. preserve every previously approved brief and artifact version;
2. identify the failed route, input version, and observable error;
3. declare which partial outputs remain valid and which downstream outputs are blocked;
4. repair or retry only at the failed dependency boundary;
5. rerun affected validators and review/publish gates;
6. deliver an explicit limitation when a required capability remains unavailable.

Never emulate a missing specialist or mark a dependent artifact complete after its prerequisite failed.

## Insufficient evidence and human handoff

When evidence is missing, stale, contradictory, below the owning skill's sufficiency bar, or requires safeguarding or professional judgment:

1. stop the affected generation path;
2. state the decision or artifact that is blocked and why the available evidence cannot support it;
3. preserve every approved brief, dependency, and partial output that remains valid;
4. name the minimum evidence or decision needed, its human owner, and the safe next action;
5. route safeguarding, legal/privacy, placement, diagnosis, or other protected judgments to the school's authorised human process;
6. resume only after the evidence or decision is recorded and the affected versions are re-approved.

Do not turn missing evidence into a model inference, generic coaching, a learner label, or a provisional student/family communication.
