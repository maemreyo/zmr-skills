# Slide Pedagogy

Possible slide purposes are hook, model, guided interaction, independent check, and close; they are not a fixed slide count. Use only purposes justified by the approved lesson. Keep one main teaching move per slide and one visible learner action when interaction is expected.

- K–2: minimal text, concrete image or teacher modelling, large targets, oral response.
- Grades 3–5: short instructions, visible scaffold, one example at a time.
- Grades 6–8: comparison, guided reasoning, and staged reveal.
- Grades 9–12: concise nuance, authentic examples, evidence and strategy.

Slides are cues and shared work surfaces, not lesson-plan paragraphs. Use generated imagery only when it improves learning; use exact source data for diagrams and charts.
