# Lifecycle and QA

Statuses: `draft` → `review` → `approved` → `retired`.

Review checks:

1. construct and objective alignment;
2. answerability and single defensible key where required;
3. distractor plausibility without trick ambiguity;
4. age, language, culture, and accessibility fit;
5. source and media authority;
6. exact and near-duplicate report;
7. spelling, grammar, numbering, option, and answer alignment;
8. blueprint distribution and exposure flags.

Deduplication is advisory. Exact normalized text scores 1.0. Near duplicates use token Jaccard as a transparent first pass; reviewers decide whether an item is a legitimate variant, an accidental duplicate, or a shared-stimulus dependency.

Record exposure outside the item body when a bank is used repeatedly. High-exposure items should be retired or isolated from high-stakes forms.
