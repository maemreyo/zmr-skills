# Teaching Brief

Capture only fields supported by the request or a labelled assumption.

- Learner: `grade_band`, age band, optional independent `cefr`, class size, confidence/profile, accessibility and support needs.
- Intent: topic, English domain, desired outcome, objective statements, success evidence, prerequisite knowledge, misconception targets, duration, delivery context.
- Language: `target_language`, `instruction_language`, bilingual balance.
- Pedagogy: requested or selected methodology, rationale, pinned status, curriculum/exam alignment, differentiation.
- Scope: lesson or unit, session count, requested artifacts, student/teacher audiences, count and length constraints.
- Authority: teacher-provided sources with required/preferred/reference status, answer authority, rubric authority.
- Constraints: must include, avoid, prohibited assumptions, privacy, difficulty, accessibility, print, and technology.
- Delivery: requested DOCX/PDF/PPTX/HTML/TSV/CSV, student/teacher surfaces, color/grayscale, and print requirements.
- Identity: `zamery`, `rooted in strength`; logo, color, and font remain unknown unless supplied.

Use provenance values `explicit`, `inferred`, `defaulted`, and `unresolved`; use `not_supplied` for optional CEFR. Authority order is: (1) safety, privacy, legal, and answer-separation hard blocks; (2) the teacher's current explicit instruction; (3) teacher sources marked required, including required answer keys or rubrics; (4) the approved Teaching Brief and blueprint; (5) teacher sources marked preferred or reference; (6) inferred values and defaults. Conflicts at levels 1–3 are surfaced; level 1 cannot be overridden, and other material conflicts require a teacher decision.

Material fields are objectives, grade band, independently supplied CEFR, methodology, assessment purpose/scope, target or instruction language, answer authority, required sources, duration, and artifact scope. Ask one question at a time only for an unresolved material field.
