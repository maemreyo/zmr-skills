# Grade and CEFR

School grade and CEFR answer different questions. Never derive a fixed CEFR from grade.

- K–2: oral-first work, phonics, concrete visuals, and 3–7 minute activity cycles.
- Grades 3–5: concrete examples, visible scaffolds, and a high guided-practice ratio.
- Grades 6–8: comparison, guided reasoning, and controlled-to-free progression.
- Grades 9–12: nuance, authentic use, exam strategy, and metalinguistic reasoning.

Use CEFR descriptors only when the teacher supplies a CEFR level or an independent assessment supports it. Otherwise keep `cefr: null` and provenance `not_supplied`. If grade and supplied CEFR suggest different scaffolds, preserve both and state the adaptation explicitly.
