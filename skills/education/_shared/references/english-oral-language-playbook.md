# English Oral Language Playbook

## Listening

- Define whether the evidence target is gist, detail, inference, stance, connected speech, or note-taking.
- Provide a reason to listen before playback.
- Use a first listen for global meaning and later listens for selected evidence.
- Teach connected-speech clues when natural speech is part of the difficulty.
- Permit learner control, transcript support, or replay only when it preserves the listening construct.

## Speaking

- Give a communicative purpose, information gap, or decision to make.
- Move from private rehearsal to pairs, small groups, and public response as appropriate.
- Separate fluency rounds from accuracy-focused repair.
- Capture observable evidence for interaction, intelligibility, language choice, and task achievement.

## Pronunciation

- Prioritise intelligibility over accent imitation.
- Combine articulatory explanation, perception discrimination, controlled production, and communicative transfer.
- Include segmental and suprasegmental evidence when relevant.

## Safety and authority

- Do not infer personality, confidence, emotion, or diagnosis from voice or participation.
- Treat recordings and transcripts as protected student evidence.
- Preserve the assessment construct when adding captions, transcripts, or replay.
