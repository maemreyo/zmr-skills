# Accessibility, Assessment, and AI Safety

## Accessibility

- Apply multiple means of engagement, representation, and action/expression without inventing learning-style categories.
- Preserve semantic heading order, reading order, alt text, contrast, keyboard access, captions, transcripts, and sufficient response space for the chosen format.
- Use only authorised accommodations and record their scope and currency.
- Keep the assessed construct unchanged unless the teacher explicitly approves a redesign.

## Assessment quality

- State the intended interpretation and use before selecting items.
- Cover each declared objective and record source authority.
- Review accessibility, bias, distractor quality, form equivalence, scoring consistency, and rater calibration where applicable.
- Label estimates and practice results honestly; do not claim certified or official status without authority.

## AI safety

- Ground factual, curriculum, answer, rubric, and source-sensitive claims in governed references.
- Separate model-generated content from teacher-approved authority.
- Require human review for scoring, placement, accommodations, learner records, and consequential decisions.
- Block PII, answer leakage, StudentCard leakage, prohibited learner labels, unsupported claims, and stale dependencies.
- Preserve audit evidence for the source, model output, human decision, and final artifact.
