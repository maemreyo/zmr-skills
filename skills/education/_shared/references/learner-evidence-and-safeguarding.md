# Learner Evidence and Safeguarding

## Evidence record

Every learner statement records a stable evidence ID, source, authority, date, context, objective when applicable, confidence for interpretations, counterevidence, expiry, consent scope, and review status.

Separate:

- observation: what was said, written, selected, or done;
- interpretation: a possible explanation with uncertainty;
- decision: a teacher-approved instructional action.

Use dated contextual descriptions. Block permanent labels such as `lazy`, `naughty`, `unmotivated`, `addicted`, `low ability`, or `problem student`.

## Student voice

Show student self-report separately from teacher interpretation. Give an age-appropriate explanation, a way to decline where applicable, and a way to challenge or correct an entry.

## Sensitive content

Hold possible serious harm, abuse, acute distress, health/diagnosis, psychological, family-conflict, income, religion, disciplinary, and other protected content outside StudentCard ingestion. Show it only to an authorised human following the school's safeguarding process. The system does not score risk or choose an intervention.

## Minimum disclosure

- Whole-class planning receives a privacy-safe aggregate ClassProfile.
- Individual teaching receives a teacher-approved, time-bounded Learner Context Snapshot.
- Content-generation and publishing skills never receive a full StudentCard.
