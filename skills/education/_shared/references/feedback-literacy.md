# Feedback Literacy

Feedback is a process that enables the learner to use evidence, not a message delivered after marking.

## Cycle

1. **Appreciate** — connect feedback to the named objective and purpose.
2. **Judge** — compare work with criteria, examples, or evidence.
3. **Manage** — acknowledge emotion without diagnosing it; keep language kind and specific.
4. **Act** — require the smallest revision, retry, or transfer response that demonstrates uptake.
5. **Review** — record what changed and whether further teaching is needed.

Marks, grades, or praise do not replace actionable feedback. Preserve student voice, accepted alternatives, authority limits, and the opportunity to retry.
